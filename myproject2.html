Introduction:  When considering what to make for my second project, I thought about something that could make my current job easier. I work for a rare coin dealer, who buys and sells rare coins. Sometimes when he goes to an auction he comes back with 50 to a hundred new coins all of which need to be photographed, then edited in photoshop for the website with a watermark, and one without a watermark to send to customers, and then save it as the correct name (the serial number) and upload it to the website. Doing three images per coin in photoshop usually would take me like 15 minutes per coin. Using this application has brought that time down to about 30 seconds. This will save a lot of time in the office and if patented could be sold to other coin dealers around the country to  make their lives easier. Basically when loading the program there is 7 arguments to provide, the first four are the paths of the images ( 2 up close of the front and back of the coin, and two further away of the front and back of the holder of the coin), the 5th is a True or False on if you want it to add a watermark to the images or not, the 6th is the name of your company to put in the watermark, and the 7th is the serial number of the coin so that each image is named after the serial number of that coin. After that, the user simply has to draw four rectangles around the front and back of the coin, and front and back of the holder, and the program does everything else for them and outputs the images with the correct name.

Workflow:

Inputs: paths to 4 images taken with camera (2 up close of front and back of coin and 2 of front and back of holder), a True or False on if you want a watermark, the name of your company, and Serial number of the coin you are doing

Example Command Line declaration:

python project2.py --image C:/project2/img9704.jpg --image2 C:/project2/img9705.jpg --hol C:/project2/img9690.jpg --holrev C:/project2/img9691.jpg --watermark True --text "John B. Hamrick & Co" --sn "06729989"

1.       Create string for watermark

2.       Create strings for names of images

3.       Check if watermark is true

4.       Read in images and resize / clone them

5.       Set up while loop and named window based on click and crop method.

6.       Let user draw rectangles around front and back of coin, press r to do over rectangle and c to crop

7.       Resize the front and reverse images to rightsize

8.       Fit onto background in the white space

9.       If watermark is true add watermark

10.   Let user draw rectangles around front and back of holder, press r to do over rectangle and c to crop

11.   Resize the front and reverse holder images to right size

12.   If watermark is true add watermark

13.   Write images to disk with serial number as name

 

Output: 3 images with serial number as name  one blue image with front and back of coin, and two holder images

 

Artistic / Technical: This project was all technical, as it is simply a program to make someones job / life easier, and to be able to support businesses to better advertise their products.
